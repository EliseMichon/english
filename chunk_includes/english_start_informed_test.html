Before going on, we would like to inform you that the syllables you have heard with the images, at the beginning of the experiment, all had a common characteristic.

This characteristic is randomly chosen for each participant from three possibilities:

(A) the vowels are associated to the first consonant of the syllables ?
For example :
"a" always after "m" and "e" always after "n" (like in "mar" and "neb").
OR
(B) the vowels are associated to the speaker of the syllables ?
For example :
"a" always pronounced by Mr Smith and "e" always pronounced by Mr Jones.
OR
(C) the vowels are associated to the intonation of the syllables ?
For example :
"a" always pronounced with a falling intonation (like in « Dot. ») and
"e" always pronounced with a rising intonation (like in « What ?»).

Note that in the syllables that you have heard with the images,
the vowels were associated to only one other property of the syllables
(consonant, speaker OR intonation).

You are now going to listen to pairs of syllables again.

Given that only one of the two syllables has the characteristic you have heard many examples of (A, B or C), for each pair, we ask you to determine which one sounds the most familiar.

Again, please do not think too much and try to base your answer on your intuition!
