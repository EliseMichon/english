Before going on, we would like to inform you that at the beginning of the experiment, you were familiarized with syllables with a common characteristic.

This characteristic is randomly chosen for each participant from three possibilities. From your point of view, in the syllables you heard with the images,

(A) the vowels were associated to the first consonant of the syllables ?
For example :
"a" always after "m" and "e" always after "n" (like in "mar" and "neb").
OR
(B) the vowels were associated to the speaker of the syllables ?
For example :
"a" always pronounced by Mr Smith and "e" always pronounced by Mr Jones.
OR
(C) the vowels were associated to the intonation of the syllables ?
For example :
"a" always pronounced with a falling intonation (like in « Dot. ») and
"e" always pronounced with a rising intonation (like in « What ?»).

Note that the vowels were associated to only one property of the syllables
(consonant (A), speaker (B) OR intonation (C)).

Now that you have more information on what you heard earlier, you are going to listen to pairs of syllables again and you will be asked to determine which one sounds the most familiar.

For each pair, only one of the two syllables has the characteristic you have heard many examples of (A, B or C).
But again, please do not think too much and try to base your answer on your intuition!
